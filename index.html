How much life insurance coverage do I need?

A general rule is to have life insurance coverage of 10-15 times your annual income. For example, if you earn ₹5 lakhs per year, aim for ₹50-75 lakhs coverage. However, this varies based on your liabilities (home loan, education loans), family size, lifestyle, and future goals. I offer free insurance need analysis for Chandigarh residents.

What is the difference between Term Insurance and Endowment Plan?

Term Insurance provides pure life coverage at low premiums with high sum assured, but no maturity benefits. Endowment Plans combine insurance with savings - you get life cover plus guaranteed returns at maturity. Term plans are best for maximum protection on a budget, while endowment plans help build wealth along with insurance coverage.

Can I get tax benefits on LIC premiums?

Yes! LIC premiums qualify for tax deduction up to ₹1.5 lakhs under Section 80C of Income Tax Act. Additionally, maturity proceeds and death benefits are tax-free under Section 10(10D). Pension plans also offer additional tax benefits under Section 80CCC. I can help you maximize your tax savings through the right LIC policy.

How do I make a claim on my LIC policy?

As your LIC agent, I provide complete claim assistance. For death claims, nominees need to submit the claim form, death certificate, policy document, and identity proof. For maturity claims, submit the discharge form and policy bond. I guide you through documentation and follow up with LIC office to ensure quick settlement within 15-30 days.

Which LIC plan is best for child's education in Chandigarh?

LIC's Jeevan Tarun, New Children's Money Back Plan, and Jeevan Lakshya are excellent for education planning. These plans provide lump sum payouts at key education milestones (10th, 12th, college) and include waiver of premium benefit - if something happens to the parent, LIC pays all future premiums. Based on your child's age and education goals, I'll recommend the perfect plan.

What is the best age to start a pension plan?

The earlier you start, the better! Starting a pension plan in your 30s or 40s allows your money to grow through compound interest, requiring lower monthly premiums. LIC offers Jeevan Akshay (immediate annuity) and Saral Pension (deferred annuity). Even if you're in your 50s, it's not too late - I can help you create a retirement corpus based on your current savings.

How do I pay my LIC premium in Chandigarh?

LIC offers multiple payment options: Online through LIC website/app, Auto-debit from bank account (ECS), Net banking, UPI, Credit/Debit card, or cash/cheque at LIC branch office.
